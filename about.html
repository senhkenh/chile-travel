Welcome to Puerto Montt & Los Lagos Region

Puerto Montt, the capital of Los Lagos Region, stands as the gateway to Chilean Patagonia and one of South America's most spectacular natural destinations. Nestled between the Pacific Ocean and the majestic Andes Mountains, this vibrant port city offers travelers an unparalleled blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventure.

Why Visit Puerto Montt?

The Los Lagos Region is renowned for its stunning volcanic landscapes, pristine lakes, and ancient temperate rainforests. Home to the iconic Osorno and Calbuco volcanoes, Lake Llanquihue (Chile's second-largest lake), and the emerald waters of Todos Los Santos Lake, this region captivates visitors with its dramatic scenery and outdoor activities year-round.

Unique Experiences

  • Volcanic Adventures: Ski on Osorno Volcano, hike through volcanic landscapes, and witness the raw power of nature
  • Lake District Exploration: Navigate crystal-clear lakes, enjoy water sports, and discover hidden beaches
  • Patagonian Gateway: Access to Chiloé Island, Carretera Austral, and the fjords of southern Chile
  • Culinary Excellence: Fresh seafood, traditional curanto, and German-influenced cuisine from historic settlements
  • Cultural Heritage: German colonial architecture, indigenous Mapuche traditions, and vibrant local markets
  • National Parks: Vicente Pérez Rosales (Chile's first national park), Alerce Andino, and Hornopirén

Best Time to Visit

Puerto Montt welcomes visitors year-round. Summer (December-March) offers warm weather perfect for hiking and water activities, while winter (June-September) transforms the region into a skiing paradise. Spring and autumn provide mild temperatures and fewer crowds, ideal for photography and nature observation.
